Indian Laws

Section 1 to Section 75: General provisions related to contracts.
Section 2(e): Definition of an agreement.
Section 10 to Section 30: Essential elements of a valid contract.
Section 62 to Section 70: Performance of contracts.
Section 73 and Section 74: Damages for breach of contract.

Sale of Goods Act, 1930:
Section 1 to Section 76: Applicability and general provisions.
Section 4 to Section 5: Sale and agreement to sell.
Section 9 to Section 15: Conditions and warranties.
Section 39 to Section 50: Performance of the contract of sale.
Section 55 to Section 61: Rights of an unpaid seller.

Transfer of Property Act, 1882:
Section 5 to Section 53A: Various modes of transferring property.
Section 54: Sale defined.
Section 123 to Section 130: Gifts.
Section 41 to Section 44: Transfer of actionable claims.

Specific Relief Act, 1963:
Section 4 to Section 25: Specific relief that may be granted.
Section 34 to Section 38: Preventive relief.
Section 41 to Section 43: Declaratory decrees.
Section 45 to Section 55: Injunctions.

Code of Civil Procedure, 1908:
Order I to Order XXXVIII: Provisions related to the parties to suits.
Order IV to Order XX: Rules regarding the institution of suits.
Order XXI: Execution of decrees and orders.

Family Laws:
Hindu Marriage Act, 1955:
Section 5 to Section 18: Conditions for a Hindu marriage.
Section 9 to Section 13: Restitution of conjugal rights and judicial separation.
Section 24 to Section 28: Maintenance.
Hindu Succession Act, 1956:
Section 6 to Section 30: Intestate succession among Hindus.
Dissolution of Muslim Marriages Act, 1939:
Sections 2 to 6: Grounds for the dissolution of Muslim marriages.

Guardians and Wards Act, 1890:
Section 1 to Section 49: Appointment of guardians for minors.

Limitation Act, 1963:
Section 2 to Section 27: Provisions related to the limitation period for different suits and appeals.

Indian Penal Code, 1860:
General Sections:
Section 299 to Section 377: Offenses against the human body.
Section 378 to Section 462: Offenses against property.
Section 463 to Section 489-E: Offenses relating to documents and property marks.
Section 489-A to Section 489-E: Offenses relating to currency notes and banknotes.
Section 490 to Section 492: Offenses against marriage.
Section 493 to Section 498: Offenses relating to abduction, adultery, and cruelty to married women.
Section 499 to Section 502: Defamation.
Section 503 to Section 510: Criminal intimidation, insult, and annoyance.

Offenses Against the State:
Section 121 to Section 130: Offenses against the state.
Section 131 to Section 140: Offenses relating to the army, navy, and air force.

Offenses Against Public Tranquility:
Section 141 to Section 160: Offenses against public tranquility.

Offenses Relating to Religion:
Section 295 to Section 298: Offenses affecting the public religious sentiments.

Offenses Against Public Health:
Section 269 to Section 294-A: Offenses against public health, safety, and convenience.

Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973:
General Sections:
Section 1 to Section 484: Provisions regarding the administration of criminal justice.
Section 154 to Section 176: Information to the police and their powers of investigation.
Section 227 to Section 231: The trial of warrant cases by the Court of Session.
Section 232 to Section 237: The trial of summons cases by Magistrates.
Section 238 to Section 259: Summary trials.

Special Provisions:
Section 376 to Section 376E: Offenses related to rape and its punishment.
Section 302: Punishment for murder.
Section 307: Attempt to murder.

Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act, 2012:
Sections 3 to 11: Offenses against children and their punishment.

Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985:
Sections 15 to 30: Offenses and penalties related to narcotics and psychotropic substances.

Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988:
Sections 7 to 13: Offenses related to corruption by public servants.

Information Technology Act, 2000:
Sections 65 to 78: Offenses related to cybercrimes.

Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015:
Sections 13 to 21: Offenses committed by children and their rehabilitation.

Article 14 to Article 32: Equality before law and equal protection of laws.
Article 19: Protection of certain rights regarding freedom of speech, assembly, association, movement, residence, and profession.
Article 21: Protection of life and personal liberty.
Article 32: Remedies for the enforcement of fundamental rights.

Article 36 to Article 51: Guidelines for the state to establish a just and egalitarian society.

Article 52 to Article 78: Powers and functions of the President, Vice President, Prime Minister, and Council of Ministers.

Amendment of the Constitution (Article 368):
Article 368: Procedure for amending the Constitution.

Election Commission of India (Article 324):
Article 324: Provides for the establishment and powers of the Election Commission for the conduct of elections.

Scheduled and Tribal Areas (Article 244 and Schedule V, VI):
Article 244 and Schedules V, VI: Special provisions for the administration of tribal areas and Scheduled Areas.
